2. The Hidden Padding (Your struct is currently 24 bytes!)
In C#, the compiler requires variables to be placed in memory at addresses that match their size. If you place a small variable before a large variable, the compiler injects "invisible" empty bytes (padding) to align the next variable. Furthermore, the total size of the struct is always rounded up to a multiple of its largest variable (which is your 8-byte ulong zobristKey).

Let's look at your current memory layout:

zobristKey (8 bytes)
depth (1 byte) + 3 bytes invisible padding
score (4 bytes)
flagType (1 byte) + 1 byte invisible padding
bestMove (2 bytes) + 4 bytes invisible padding at the end
Because of the mixed order, your Transposition struct is currently bloated to 24 bytes per entry! This means your 64MB Transposition Table is actually allocating 100MB in RAM!
